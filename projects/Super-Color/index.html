Abstract

The distribution of colors in an image greatly influences viewer perception and is often expertly manipulated in various visual mediums like films and video games to guide focus, enhance narratives, and create emotion. In contrast, creating such color-focused images requires superior expertise, as it involves a complex process of identifying specific areas to emphasize and manually modifying the color properties to augment the image's overall impact and expressiveness. To overcome this challenge, we present Super-Color, a novel technique to colorize gray-scale images with color focus automatically. We propose a color-focus extraction approach to identify the potential focus regions of gray-scale images. We introduce pre-trained image encoding models to extract color-focus information to obtain a more general focus extraction capability. Then, we explore the ability of transformation image generation capability of text-to-image generation models into colorization task. We design a conditional perceptual autoencoder to avoid unfaithful results, and we analyze the existing popular tuning of conditional diffusion models and give a tuning paradigm for diffusion models under the colorization task. Finally, we propose a color enhancement technique to balance preserving original aesthetics and enhancing key focus areas. Extensive experiments affirm that our colorization backbone surpasses the performance of state-of-the-art methods. Further user studies and application exploration show that our Super-Color framework generates high-quality and visually pleasing colorization results, which cannot be achieved with general colorization models. We will release our code upon the acceptance of this paper.
