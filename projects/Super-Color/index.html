Super-Color: Let There Be Focus

Xiangqiao Meng1,2 Lei He 2  Tong-Yee Lee 3  Ping Li 1 
1 The Hong Kong Polytechnic University, Hong Kong
2 Eastern Institute for Advanced Study, Ningbo, Zhejiang, China
3 National Cheng Kung University, Taiwan

Abstract

The distribution of colors in an image greatly influences viewer perception and is often expertly manipulated in various visual mediums like films and video games to guide focus, enhance narratives, and create emotion. In contrast, creating such color-focused images requires superior expertise, as it involves a complex process of identifying specific areas to emphasize and manually modifying the color properties to augment the image's overall impact and expressiveness. To overcome this challenge, we present Super-Color, a novel technique to colorize gray-scale images with color focus automatically. Initially, we propose a color-focus extraction approach to identify the potential focus regions of gray-scale images. Common salient object detection methods are inapplicable to our task, the limited training dataset makes the domain gap with real colorization scene. We introduce image classification models to extract visual attention information to obtain a more general focus extraction capability. Then, we propose colorization-LDM to transfer the image generation capability of text-to-image generation models into the colorization task. We design a new latent gray-scale image encoder to guide colorization with multi-level feature injection and a conditional perceptual autoencoder to avoid unfaithful results. Finally, we propose a color enhancement technique to balance preserving original aesthetics and enhancing key focus areas. Extensive experiments affirm that our colorization backbone surpasses the performance of state-of-the-art methods. Further user studies and application exploration show that our \textit{Super-Color} framework generates high-quality and visually pleasing colorization results, which cannot be achieved with general colorization models.
